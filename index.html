Pull Day — Lower Body Focus

Full body Pull Day = the whole back of your body! Back, glutes, hamstrings, and biceps.

Today's reminders:

✓ Set your arches — feel the arch in your foot before every set. It makes your glutes and hamstrings fire way harder.

✓ Breathe and brace — big breath into your belly before each rep. Tight core = stronger everything.

✓ Squeeze your shoulder blades — on every pull, think about squeezing a pencil between your shoulder blades. That's how you actually build your back instead of just using your arms.

✓ Slow down — if you're rushing, the right muscles aren't doing the work. The slower you go, the more you grow.

Full body Push Day = chest, shoulders, triceps, and core-loaded legs.

Today's reminders:

✓ Set your arches — even on pressing days, your feet are your foundation. Arch set = more power from the ground up.

03

Pull Day — Upper Body Focus

Back and biceps are the stars today. Lower body gets core-loaded glute and hamstring work.

Today's reminders:

✓ Set your arches — especially on single-leg moves. The arch controls your knee position which controls whether the right muscles fire.

✓ Breathe and brace — breathe in through your nose, feel your ribs expand sideways. That's your core locking in.

✓ Pull with your back, not your arms — on every row and pulldown, initiate by squeezing your shoulder blade first, THEN bend your elbow. If your biceps are burning but your back isn't — you're doing it wrong.

✓ Quality reps only — the moment your form breaks, the set is over. More reps with bad form just trains bad form.

04

Push Day — Lower Body Focus

Legs are the main event. Squats, lunges, and core-loaded pressing to finish.

Today's reminders:

✓ Set your arches — before every squat and lunge, build the arch. Feel it spiral your knees outward. That's the position where your quads and glutes actually engage fully.

✓ Breathe and brace — big belly breath before you go down, brace like someone's about to poke you in the stomach. That's your built-in weight belt.

✓ Knees track over toes — don't let your knees cave in. Push them out over your pinky toes. This is how the glutes do their job.

✓ Earn every rep — today has some new, challenging moves. It's okay if they're hard. Go slow, use less weight, and own the position. The strength comes from the form, not the load.
